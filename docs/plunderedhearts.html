Sample Question

There's a bird nearby with a jewel in its beak. How can I get it?

A.You can't catch the bird, but you can make it drop the jewel.
B.What do birds like to eat?
C.TAKE THE WORM FROM THE FISHERMAN. If you then DROP THE WORM, the bird
D.will drop the jewel.

The Ship

Good Gracious! What is this extremely improper scene at the very beginning?

A.You are asleep, dreaming this when the pirates attack.
B.It's a preview of things to come.
C.It's a sample of the writing style of _Plundered Hearts_.
D.Romance novels always have teasers of this sort.

Pirates are attacking _Lafond Deux_! What can I do?

A.You don't need to do anything, actually, though you may like to whack
B.an occasional pirate over the head with something. Heroines always get
C.rescued from ticklish situations like this one. Well, almost always.

I'm stuck in the Captain's Quarters! How can I escape?

A.There are two ways to escape, actually. One to the fore and one abaft.
B.Have you looked behind the curtain?
C.The cupboard isn't quite blocking the doorway.
D.To go to the fore, type WALK FORWARD. If Captain Jamison is present, he
E.won't let you go, of course.
F.To go aft, see the next question ("How do I get on deck?").

How do I get on deck?

A.The hatch is closed tight and the door is barred, so you'll have to
B.find some other way.
C.You need to climb the rope ladder that appears when Jamison leaves the
D.ship.
E.The window won't open, but there's another way to "open" it.
F.THROW THE COFFER AT THE WINDOW.
G.Having broken the window, you can't reach the ladder without getting on
H.the ledge first. SIT ON THE LEDGE.
I.All you need do is TAKE THE LADDER. Then you'll have to climb it
J.quickly. You can't hang onto slimy hemp forever.
K.With practice, climbing this ladder becomes much easier. In fact,
L.after you've done it once, a simple CLIMB UP and CLIMB DOWN will
M.suffice.
N.
O.I can't climb the ladder! My skirts get in the way!
P.Climb on to the ledge again, remove your dress and start the process
Q.over. This is no time for modesty.
R.
S.Immodest I must be to get on deck, but I cannot, so undressed, preserve my

virtue from Jamison's lusty crew. How may I move around?

A.Is there some way to disguise your gender?
B.Have you thoroughly examined the sleeping cupboard in the landing?
C.Opened it? Climbed into it? Examined the pile of clothes in it?
D.If you wear the shirt and breeches as a disguise, the pirates won't
E.bother you.

How can I prevent the ship from blowing up?

A.You need to get on deck first.
B.Did you notice, in the hold, the fuse burning towards the kegs of
C.gunpowder, behind the fence?
D.Since you can't get through the fence yourself, you have to discover
E.some way of putting the fuse out from a distance.
F.The gap above the fence will help.
G.You can throw things over the fence on to the burning end of the fuse.
H.They just slide off, you say, leaving the fuse undoused? What best
I.fights fire?
J.Water! But since you can't move the water barrel from the deck, you'll
K.have to rip off some other solution.
L.Getting ragged thinking about this?
M.RIP THE FROCK to make a rag. Then SOAK THE RAG IN THE WATER.
N.Finally, THROW THE WET RAG AT THE FUSE.

How can I stop the _Helena Louise_ from dashing on the rocks?

A.You need to get on deck first.
B.You may have overheard Crulley committing some mischief on the deck
C.above the crew's quarters. That's the forecastle.
D.Have you gone north from the forecastle to the bowsprit?
E.One of the two anchor lines has been cut. The ship is drifting on the
F.tide.
G.You'll have to lower another anchor. Know what a mooring winch is?
H.Examine the lever on the winch.
I.To open the teeth on the winch, in order to release the main anchor,
J.RAISE THE LEVER.

How can I rescue my father, trapped as I am aboard the _Helena Louise_?

A.To escape the ship, you need to get on deck first.
B.Have you examined the pyramid of casks on the quarterdeck?
C.One of the casks is open ended.
D.If you enter the cask, you will notice...
E.A fraying rope. Maybe if you just helped it along a little.
F.There's a dagger in the Galley.
G.ENTER THE CASK. CUT THE ROPE WITH THE DAGGER.

I escape the ship but drift out to sea. How can I get ashore?

A.The tide is going out. Wait for the tide to change.
B.While you're waiting for the tide to change, you might want to stop the
C._Helena Louise_ from dashing against the reefs.

Of what use is the skiff?

A.You never HAVE to use it, it is just there in case you want to go back
B.to the _Helena Louise_...
C.To get items you forgot to bring along...
D.To go back to save the ship from exploding.

The Mansion

How can I get into the mansion?

A.There are five routes into the house, two from the western side and one
B.from every other direction.
C.The easterly entrance is approached from the Folly. See the question
D.about the slat in the folly for more information. ("There is a loose
E.slat at the back of the folly. Is it important?")
F.One of the western entrances is from the Trade Entrance, into the
G.kitchen. See the next question for more information. ("Who is this
H.woman who keeps throwing me out of the kitchen?")
I.Another is from the Clearing northeast of the Forest. The place with
J.the climbing vines.
K.When you climb the vine in the clearing, you'll find your way into an
L.upstairs room.
M.The northerly entrance is south from the Main Entrance. You'll need to
N.have gotten something from inside the house (entering by another route)
O.before you can successfully enter here, however.
P.The final entrance is through the doors between the veranda and the
Q.ballroom. You'll have to unlock and open them from inside the ballroom
R.first, however.
S.
T.Who is this woman who keeps throwing me out of the kitchen? How can I make

her stop?

A.Her name is Lucy, and she is a friend of your father's. You'll probably
B.want to talk to her.
C.Lucy hasn't time to chat with beggars (your dress is looking pretty
D.bedraggled without its hoops), or with fine ladies. Your boyish
E.disguise could encourage her to chat.
F.Enter the kitchen wearing the shirt and breeches.
G.Remember to take the garter when Lucy offers it to you. It might be
H.useful.

There is a loose slat at the back of the folly. Is it important?

A.If you MOVE THE SLAT, a hole will be formed.
B.If you GO THROUGH THE HOLE, you'll find a back entrance into the
C.mansion.

How do I get the pistols in the Parlour?

A.You can't.
B.Really. You can't. So stop trying.

The Ball

I haven't been to a ball in so long! How can I get in?

A.Since the veranda doors are locked, you'll have to enter past the
B.butler in the foyer.
C.Of course, if you don't look like a guest, the butler won't even let
D.you in the house. You'll have to dress properly.
E.Enter the foyer wearing a ball gown.
F.You'll also need an invitation to give to the butler.
G.
H.I don't have an invitation to the ball!
I.If you haven't found an invitation, you obviously did not examine the
J.coffer thoroughly before losing it to the sea. (Don't worry. This isn't
K.necessary to win the game.)
L.If you found the invitation, but left it aboard the _Helena Louise_,
M.you could climb into the skiff and row out to the ship, get the
N.invitation and then row back. This would work, but...
O.The butler will take other compensation for your loss of the
P.invitation.
Q.BRIBE THE BUTLER WITH THE BANKNOTE. No thug is above a little bribery.
R.
S.I'm Cinderella, with nothing to wear to the ball! Where's my fairy

godmother?

A.This is not a fairy tale! This is real life! (Well, as close to it as
B.romance and interactive fiction ever come.)
C.Have you found the Clearing yet?
D.The Clearing is northeast of the forest. There are climbing vines
E.there.
F.If you climb up the vines, you can crawl into the second floor window.
G.That which you seek is in that bedroom.
H.
I.Lafond is threatening to kill my father unless I dance with him. What shall

I do?

A.Dance with Lafond.
B.Unless you've already rescued your father, in which case you may try to
C.refuse him.
D.But Lafond is not a man to toy with.

Rescuing Papa

Jamison can't find Papa anywhere, nor can I. Where else should I look?

A.Jamison has a theory about where a dungeon might be. Dance with him to
B.find out.
C.The Library is in the east wing of the house. Is there anything
D.suspicious in the Library?
E.Jean Lafond is depicted in the portrait in the Library in exactly the
F.same pose as he is on the banknote. Examine the banknote that came in
G.your game package for clues.
H.Notice what Lafond is carrying.
I.Notice what Lafond is wearing.
J.Notice what Lafond is doing. This must be done last. Voila!
K.Once you have done this you may simply CLOSE and OPEN THE PORTRAIT.
L.
M.Lafond said he'd send for me when he was ready. I've been waiting around for

hours and nothing has happened. What's taking so long?

A.Lafond is a busy man, with many things to take care of before he can
B.relax. You should fill the extra time he's allowing you with the
C.discovery and rescue of your father.

What do I do to get by the crocodile?

A.The object is to render the beast incapable of biting you.
B.There are two ways, actually.
C.One way involves the garter. Go to Clue H for that solution.
D.Another way involves the laudanum you found in the Crew's Quarters.
E.You'll have to feed the crocodile something that has been doped with
F.laudanum. Food would be best.
G.There's something edible (to crocodiles) in the bottom of the cask in
H.which you floated to shore.
I.SQUEEZE THE LAUDANUM ON THE PORK. FEED THE PORK TO THE CROCODILE.
J.The first clue for the alternate solution requires a brief foray into
K.zoology: Crocodiles, while able to snap a log in two with their jaws,
L.have weak muscles for opening those very same jaws. Thus, a strong man
M.is capable of holding their jaws shut with his bare hands.
N.Since you are neither a strong man nor able to sit around holding
O.closed the monster's mouth, you have to find something else able to
P.hold it shut for you.
Q.The garter is a likely candidate.
R.Throw something at the reptile to get it to snap shut its jaws, then
S.PUT THE GARTER ON THE CROCODILE.
T.
U.Here's a locked cell and I have no key.
V.Have you thoroughly searched the whole dungeon area?
W.Have you been in the Guard's Cell?
X.The key is on the cot in the Guard's Cell.

Robert's fallen into the quicksand! How can I save him?

A.Look around for a large branch.
B.Get the fallen branch in the forest.
C.Drop the branch into the quicksand for Robert to grab hold of...
D.But this probably won't work since there's no branch, no quicksand and
E.no Robert in this game.

Lafond's Bedroom

Where is Lafond's room?

A.It is through the door in the north wall of the upstairs east hallway.
B.It is also the only room on the second story with a window facing
C.seaward.
D.
E.How can I escape Lafond's clutches? I'm in his room, helpless and terrified.
F.DRINK THE WINE whenever Lafond tells you to.
G.Make sure there's nothing in the wine you drink, however.
H.Ever hear the ditty, "The pellet of the poison's in the chalice from
I.the palace,/ The vessel with the pestle has the brew that is true"?
J.POUR WINE INTO THE GREEN GOBLET. Then put something else into it.
K.SQUEEZE LAUDANUM INTO THE GREEN GOBLET. When Lafond is busy on the
L.other side of the room, obviously.
M.But Lafond knows you're doing something! So double-cross the devious
N.dog!
O.Lafond realizes that you've tampered with at least one of the glasses,
P.so he pretends to have forgotten which is his. If you type YES to his
Q.question (If the green goblet is his?) then he'll assume you poisoned
R.that glass, since you want him to drink from it. If you type NO, then
S.he'll assume you drugged the other (blue glass). Then, being the cruel
T.tyrant he is, he'll force you to drink from whichever glass he thinks
U.you want him to drink from. Of course, if you lie...
V.When Lafond asks if the green goblet is his, type NO if you have put
W.the laudanum in it. (If you've drugged the blue goblet, type YES.)
X.Then, when he forces you to drink the wine you indicated, imbibe "the
Y.brew that is true." This leaves the poisoned wine for...
Z.The butler, not Lafond, unfortunately. So, it doesn't quite work the
[.way you planned; at least the butler is taken care of.
\.

^.This is a spicy puzzle.
_.Have you thoroughly examined the chicken and spices?

a.They are HOT!
b.TAKE THE PEPPER THEN BLOW THE PEPPER AT LAFOND.

d.I've gotten rid of Lafond, but the butler won't let me leave the room!
e.SQUEEZE THE LAUDANUM into one of the goblets, then POUR THE WINE into
f.that goblet.
g.WALK SOUTH. The butler will think you're trying to escape, and follow
h.you back into the room.
i.GIVE THE (drugged) GOBLET TO THE BUTLER. No thug is above a little

k.WAIT. WAIT. WAIT. It takes a little while for the drug to take effect.

m.
n.I've made it out of Lafond's room, but he catches me afterwards!
o.In such situations, one should get some outside help.
p.Cookie and the crew aboard Jamison's ship might provide some
q.assistance.
r.Signal the ship from an eastward-looking window.
s.Wave the mirror in the moonbeam shining through the window of Lafond's
t.room. The pirates will respond.
u.If you've lost the mirror, you can use the silver tray.

Rescuing Nicholas

(If you haven't been in Lafond's room, don't develop these.)

Oh no! I can't get past this crocodile again! In such situations, one should get some outside help. Cookie and the crew aboard Jamison's ship might provide some assistance. Signal the ship from an eastward-looking window. Wave the mirror in the moonbeam shining in the window of Lafond's room. The pirates will respond. If you've lost the mirror, you can use the silver tray. If you lead Cookie to the crocodile, he'll take care of the beast for you.

The Endgame

They're duelling in the ballroom! How can I stop them?! First of all, get to someplace where you can see the duel. Now that you're in the Gallery, do you see anyway to transport yourself down to the dance floor? The rope! This is a pirate story! What do pirates ALWAYS do?! (Besides rape, pillage and plunder.) Apes do it, monkeys do it, even Tarzan of the Jungle. You do it, you... SWING ON THE ROPE. Untie the rope first.

Explanations of the Four Different Conclusions

The four possible final ranks are: Love Transcending Death. You can get this rank if you FIRE THE PISTOL INTO THE AIR. Orphan. If you put the stone into the garter and sling it at Crulley. Pirate Queen. Climb into the skiff as soon as you get to the beach, and row towards the ship. Happily Ever After. SHOOT THE PIRATE with the loaded pistol.

How the Points Are Scored

One point for each of the following actions:

TAKE PILE OF CLOTHES TAKE BOTTLE THROW COFFER THROUGH WINDOW ENTER POOP FROM LADDER (the first time) RAISE LEVER (on winch) THROW (WET) RAG OVER GATE CUT LINE WITH DAGGER WEAR BEAUTIFUL BALL GOWN GIVE INVITE TO BUTLER or GIVE BANKNOTE TO BUTLER TAKE TREATISE OF POWER (first time) OPEN PORTRAIT (first time) TAKE KEY (first time) Drugging the croc or MUZZLE THE CROC WALK NORTH (enter Dad's cell) DANCE WITH LAFOND (when he invites you to his room) WALK NORTH (enter Lafond's room) Drugging the butler BLOW SPICES AT LAFOND SIGNAL SHIP WITH MIRROR or TRAY ATTACK CRULLEY WITH RAPIER (tripping him down the well) REVIVE NICK WITH SALTS PICK LOCK (on manacles) SWING ON ROPE LOAD PISTOL WITH GUNPOWDER or PUT STONE IN GARTER SLING STONE (in garter) AT CRULLEY or FIRE PISTOL or SHOOT THE PIRATE [with the pistol] or Jump in the skiff and row off in the end scene.
